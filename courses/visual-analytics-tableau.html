Visual Analytics with Tableau

  • Overview
  • Curriculum
  • Instructor

Tableau is a market leading visual analytics platform used to aid data driven decision making in organizations across a variety of industries. For modern day visual storytelling, it is the go-to platform as it is highly sophisticated & easy to use compared to its competitors.

At Hyreblock, we offer a comprehensive Tableau package for learners that comprises training, projects, help on creating a portfolio, and professional mentorship. Our team with trainers having relevant experience will work with you on certain strategic goals of your career during the engagement period.

The course is designed by industry experts who have relevant experience in solving complex business problems using Tableau. Also, there are dedicated sessions to address Q&A or feedback from learners.

  • The course can be conducted on weekends or weekdays (flexible as per your schedule).
  • At the end of this course, all learners will be aware of key Tableau concepts & capabilities.
  • Tableau public portfolio with multiple Tableau dashboards developed by you.
  • Certificate on blockchain with badge based on performance.

Course Schedule

To learn from basics of Tableau to developing a comprehensive dashboard with clean design in 1 month The course is structured to have both classroom learning and hands-on projects in a cohort-based learning environment.

  • Tableau Concepts

    • Dimensions & measures, Discrete & continuous fields, Aggregation & granularity, Ratio calculations, connecting to & preparing data connections, join, union, blend, & managing data properties

  • Visualization I

    • Bar chart, line chart, scatterplot, filled map, combined axis chart, dual axis chart, stacked bar chart, crosstab, highlight table

  • Filtering, grouping & sorting datas

    • Filters - context, dimension, measure, extract, data source, date part & value, dates – discrete & continuous, groups – visual, labels, fields, sets – static & dynamic, hierarchies, sorting – manual, nested & computed

  • Calculated fields, parameters & trends

    • Trendlines & reference lines, table calculations – type, scope & direction, bins and histograms, calculated fields, parameters – usage with sets, histograms & reference lines

  • Presentation & Interactivity

    • Formatting – fonts, coloring, size, custom shapes, layouts, actions – filter, highlight, go to URL, mobile & tablet view, Storytelling with story points, Share as PDF or image

  • Data connections

    • Options – multiple data sources, single & multiple databases, Metadata grid, Pivot, Data interpreter, Relationships, Logical & physical layers, Joins vs relationships, Data extract formats and capabilities

  • Visualization II

    • Fields & chart types (Tableau-generated fields, How & when to build: Word cloud, Heat maps, Tree maps, Symbol maps, Pie charts, Bullet graphs), Animations (sequential & simultaneous)

  • Advanced analytics

    • Reference bands, Trend model, Forecasting, Reference distributions, Statistical summary, Data highlighter, Actions – go to sheet, change parameter, change set values, LOD - INCLUDE, EXCLUDE, FIXED

  • Calculations

    • Tooltip visualizations, labels & axes, annotations, calculations & usage - string, arithmetic & date, quick table & ad-hoc calculations, logic statements, aggregation, Grand totals & sub-totals, Join calculations

  • Geospatial analytics

    • Map navigation - pan & zoom, filtering, layering, lasso & radial selection, geographic search, blend geographical data, spatial files, pagination, row level security
